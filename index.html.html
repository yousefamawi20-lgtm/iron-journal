Training Principles
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5 kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks. For isolation moves, add reps first, then weight. This is the single biggest driver of results.
Round 1 vs Round 2
Round 1 (3 days) focuses on strength with heavier loads and lower reps. Round 2 (3 days) focuses on hypertrophy with higher reps and pump volume. Both hit each muscle once — so every muscle gets trained twice weekly.
Warm-Up Protocol
5 min cardio + 2 warm-up sets at 50% and 70% of your working weight before the first compound of each day.
Form Over Ego
As a beginner, master the movement pattern before chasing weight. Film yourself on compounds to check form.
Nutrition
1.6–2.2 g protein per kg of bodyweight daily. Eat in a slight calorie surplus (+200–300 kcal) to build muscle. Hydrate (3+ litres water).
Sleep is Training
Muscles grow during rest, not during the workout. Aim for 7–9 hours per night. Growth hormone peaks in deep sleep.
Deload Week
After 6–8 weeks of consistent training, take a deload week — reduce volume and intensity by 40–50%. You'll come back stronger.
Data Storage
All your weights and progress are saved locally in this browser. Don't clear browser data or you'll lose your log. For long-term tracking, consider a dedicated app like Hevy or Strong.
