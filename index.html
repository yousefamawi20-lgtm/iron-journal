Training Principles
Why Upper / Lower?
Each muscle group is trained twice per week — the research-backed minimum for maximum hypertrophy. Round A is heavier (strength), Round B is higher volume (size). You get both adaptations every week.
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5 kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks. For isolation, add reps first (until you hit the top of the rep range), then add weight. This is the single biggest driver of results.
Rep Tempo
Use a 2-1-2 tempo: 2 seconds lowering, 1 second pause, 2 seconds lifting. Control the weight — don't swing or bounce.
Warm-Up
5 min light cardio + 2 warm-up sets (50% and 70% of your working weight) before your first compound lift of the day.
Protein & Nutrition
Aim for 1.6–2.2 g protein per kg of bodyweight daily (you're 82 kg, so ~130–180 g/day). Slight caloric surplus of 200–300 kcal to support muscle gain. Drink 3+ litres of water daily.
Sleep Is Training
Muscles grow during rest, not during the workout. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Aim for 7–9 hours per night — especially on training days.
Rest Timer
Expand any exercise card and tap a rest timer button after your set. The timer runs at the top of the screen.
Deload
After completing this month, take a deload week — reduce weight and volume by 40%. You'll come back noticeably stronger.
